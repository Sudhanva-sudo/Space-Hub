About Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its iconic Great Red Spot and numerous moons. It is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter has a powerful magnetic field and is home to some of the most fascinating weather patterns and storms in the solar system.

Discoveries about Jupiter

Jupiter has been the subject of many scientific discoveries over the years, starting with Galileo's first observation of its moons in 1610. NASA’s Juno mission has provided new insights into its atmosphere, magnetic field, and deep interior.

  • In 1610, Galileo discovered Jupiter’s four largest moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
  • The Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth, has been observed for over 350 years.
  • NASA's Juno mission, launched in 2011, has revealed new data on Jupiter’s magnetic field, auroras, and polar regions.
