About Mars Science Laboratory

The Mars Science Laboratory (MSL) mission is a project designed by NASA with the goal of landing a rover, Curiosity, on Mars to explore the surface. The mission seeks to investigate whether the Martian environment has ever been capable of supporting microbial life. Curiosity is equipped with a suite of scientific instruments to study the planet's climate and geology, and search for signs of past life.

Mission Goals

  • Determine whether life ever arose on Mars.
  • Study the climate of Mars.
  • Analyze the geology of the Red Planet.
  • Prepare for human exploration of Mars.
